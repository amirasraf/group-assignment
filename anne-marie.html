Anne Marie

Anne-Marie Rose Nicholson was born in April 7, 1991. Anne-Marie is an English singer and was born and reared in Essex's East Tilbury. Her father is a carpenter and handyman from London's East End, and her mother is a teacher in Essex. She has one sister named Samantha, and as a youngster, she featured in two West End productions: "Les Misérables" when she was six, and Whistle Down the Wind, co-starring Jessie J, when she was twelve.



Anne-Marie is a Shotokan karate black belt. She began karate instruction when she was nine years old. She earned Double Gold at the Funakoshi Shotokan Karate Association World Championships in 2002, Gold and Silver at the Funakoshi Shotokan Karate Association World Championships in 2007, and Gold at the United Kingdom Traditional Karate Federation National Championships. She attributes this to teaching her "discipline and self-control." Now, she doesn’t have time to practice it frequently due to her performing commitment.



She has charted tracks on the UK Singles Chart, including Clean Bandit's "Rockabye," which peaked at number one with Sean Paul, as well as "Alarm,", "Ciao Adios," "Friends," "2002," and "Don't Play." Speak Your Mind, her debut studio album, was released on April 27, 2018, and reached at number three on the UK Albums Chart. She was nominated for four Brit Awards in 2019, including Best British Female Solo Artist. She obtained a record deal with Asylum in 2015 and began releasing her music through the label. She has been a coach on The Voice UK since 2021.
