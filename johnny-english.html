JOHNNY ENGLISH

Johnny English is a spy comedy film directed by Peter Howitt and written by Neal Purvis, Robert Wade, and William Davies that was released in 2003. It's a British-French collaboration produced by StudioCanal and Working Title Films, and it's released by Universal Pictures.



It is the first part in the Johnny English film series and acts as a comedy and tribute to the spy genre, namely the James Bond film series, as well as Atkinson's Mr. Bean character. It stars Rowan Atkinson in the starring role, Natalie Imbruglia, Ben Miller, and John Malkovich. The character is also connected to Atkinson's clumsy spy persona from a series of commercials for Barclaycard in the 1990s in the United Kingdom.



Jonny English was released theatrically in the United States on July 18, 2003. The movie received mixed reviews from critics but was a commercial success, generating $160 million worldwide against a $40 million budget. The film was released in the United Kingdom on April 11, 2003, and dominated the box office for the next three weekends before being surpassed by X2. Johnny English Reborn (2011) and Johnny English Strikes Again (2018) were the two sequels to the Johnny English.
