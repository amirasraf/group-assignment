TWENTY ØNE PILØT

Twenty One Pilot is a Columbus, Ohio-based musical duo with Tyler Joseph as the band's lead vocalist. They established the band in 2009 alongside Nick Thomas and Chris Salih, who both left in 2011. Since their departure, the band's lineup has been made up of Joseph and drummer Josh Dun. The singles "Stressed Out," "Ride," and "Heathens" among the duo's most well-known. At the 59th Annual Grammy Awards, they won a Grammy for Best Pop Duo/Group Performance for "Stressed Out."



Before being signed by record company Fueled by Ramen in 2012, the band self-released two albums, Twenty One Pilots (2009) and Regional at Best (2011). Their label debut called "Vessel" was released in 2013 and became just the second album in history to have every track obtain at least a gold certification and making Twenty One Pilots the first band in music history to have every song on two albums gain gold or platinum certification.



The duo's fourth album, Blurryface that were released in 2015, spawned the popular songs "Stressed Out" and "Ride" and became the first album to gain at least a gold certification from the Recording Industry Association of America. With the release of "Heathens," the group became the first alternative artist in history to have two concurrent top five songs on the Billboard Hot 100, as well as the third rock act in history to have two singles concurrently chart in the top five on the Billboard Hot 100. This mighty duo has released six studio albums to far, the most recent being Scaled and Icy, which was released on May 21, 2021. They also have nine No. 1 singles on the Alternative Songs list, making them the artist with the sixth-most No. 1 songs on the chart.
